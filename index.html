Paradiso

August 8, 2020

I found this under my mattress. In case you do not know (of course), all of my old artworks are stored under my mattress (including cringe worthy artworks). I also stored my past artworks that I made when I was still in middle school and junior high school (grades 8, 9, and 10). I used to join art competitions before and I even made it up to the nationals and I won the competition hahaha! (akalain mo 'yun) So, back to this artwork. This is just one of my "best" practice artwork before I joined the nationals. I know it is not as perfect as Leonardo DaVinci's works, but for me, this is my favorite "practice artwork". Why? Because I have to practice every day for more than 3 months just to master how oil pastels blend. On my last day of practice with Mr. C (my art mentor), we practiced really hard and slept really late. I ended up waking up late as well on the day of my competition hahaha! I love reminiscing my journey in Woodridge College. I had a lot of fun and weird experiences.

The Ocean Owns Me

August 6, 2020

I made this last July 30, 2020. I used to have a lot of paintings, but this painting is the only one left. I used to paint a lot before I entered the IT industry. I still find this hobby fun and relaxing, but since I am now focusing in learning software engineering I eventually stopped painting random portraits. I painted this last week because I was not feeling good or stable. Quarantine made me realized a lot of things and my head was having a lot of random thoughts (negative + positive thoughts). I am not good in expressing myself through writing paragraphs and essays that's why painting surrealistic artworks is my way of telling people how I feel.
